Games

Pulse Fighters


Pulse Fighters is a 4-Player Arcade-Style-Brawler that reacts and changes depending on your heart rate. It included a pulse sensor, 3D printed controllers and the us of a wireless mobile app controller that allows anyone to jump in and play without needing anything but a phone or tablet.

As lead programmer on the project, I created the game engine and UI functionality, while also assisting with networking and many other aspects, including models and texturing.

It was created along with five other team members for the Carleton University Interactive Multimedia and Design Senior Project Fair. More can be found at our website.
Puppet Paradise


Puppet Paradise is a sandbox exploration game aimed at younger children. Using the Leap Motion Plus hand tracking technology, the player controls a puppet with their thumbs and forefingers, allowing them to move about and interact with the game world.

As lead programmer on this project, I created the game engine and integrated the Leap Motion API, as well as assited in 3D models and game design.

With two other team members, this game was created for a class at Carleton University. More can be found at our website.
